Cross-National Research Study

The Role of AI in Education

Research Thesis

The Inquiry

A cross-national study examining how high school students use AI and its effects on learning, skills, and academic ethics. Surveying 217 students across 42 countries, the research revealed that AI is widely integrated into daily learning, improving writing and comprehension, but raising critical questions about dependency.

Core Finding

The risk lies not in the tool itself, but in the decline of critical thinking when overreliance occurs. The study highlighted the urgent need for AI literacy and policies that balance technological benefits with essential skill development.

Reflective Outcome

"This research reinforced my understanding of how technology can both empower and challenge education. It underscored the responsibility required to integrate these tools thoughtfully—ensuring they serve as a catalyst for growth rather than a replacement for thought."

Community Initiative

STEM Pathmakers

Founder

The Context

Realizing that access to STEM education was uneven and information about opportunities was often hard to find, I founded STEM Pathmakers to create a supportive community where young learners could explore STEM confidently.

The Action

Through tutoring, mentorship, and workshops, the initiative helps students understand challenging concepts, navigate academic pathways, and discover their potential in science, technology, engineering, and math.

Reflective Outcome

"Leading STEM Pathmakers taught me how equitable access to knowledge can empower others and build confidence in ways that extend far beyond the classroom. It reinforced my commitment to social responsibility through educational equity."

Project 01

Agro AI

2025

The Context

A planning tool using satellite imagery, soil data, and machine learning to guide farmers on what and when to plant. Designed for unreliable internet environments, it transforms complex data into practical advice.

The Challenge

Balancing technological precision with real-world usability, ensuring sophisticated insights remained accessible to those whose livelihoods depend on the land.

Reflective Outcome

"This project showed me how data and local knowledge must come together to support sustainable agriculture. It reinforced my belief that powerful technologies must listen to the community."

Sustainability Machine Learning
Project 02

AfriCare

2025

The Context

An AI-powered health companion making healthcare accessible in underserved regions. It addresses language barriers and poor internet through symptom insights, telemedicine, and multilingual record management.

The Challenge

The real challenge wasn’t building the technology; it was making it usable and meaningful for people with vastly different levels of digital literacy and diverse cultural backgrounds.

Reflective Outcome

"Through this project, I learned how technology can empower communities and bridge gaps in access while respecting local contexts—bridging education and care where it's needed most."

Public Health Multilingual AI
Project 03

HerGuardian

2025

The Context

A safety app helping women navigate public spaces using real-time location tracking and anonymous reporting to identify safe and unsafe areas within a community.

The Challenge

Translating complex geolocation and incident data into clear, actionable guidance that provides safety without compromising user privacy.

Reflective Outcome

"HerGuardian showed me how design decisions can empower users in meaningful ways, supporting personal safety while strictly respecting privacy and social context."
